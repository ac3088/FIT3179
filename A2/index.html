The Impact of Natural Disasters

Per IFRC, "Disasters are serious disruptions to the functioning of a community that exceed its capacity to cope using its own resources."

Natural disasters are those that are caused by severe weather or climate events, or are in some way related to a natural phenomenon, rather than a man-made one.

Storms and floods are the most common natural disasters, accounting for over 50% of reported natural disasters since 1960.

Note that these statistics are not reflected in the above map, as locational data for many natural disasters(notably storms) was not available.

In total, these disasters have caused nearly 4 trillion USD in damages, and taken over 5 million lives.
